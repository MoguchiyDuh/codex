# Exponents and Powers

Exponents represent repeated multiplication and are fundamental to algebra, calculus, and many scientific applications.

---

## Definition

An **exponent** (or **power**) indicates how many times a base number is multiplied by itself.

$$\boxed{a^n = \underbrace{a \cdot a \cdot a \cdot \ldots \cdot a}_{n \text{ times}}}$$

Where:
- $a$ is the **base**
- $n$ is the **exponent** (or **power**)
- $a^n$ is read as "$a$ to the power of $n$" or "$a$ raised to the $n$-th power"

**Examples:**
- $2^5 = 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 = 32$
- $3^4 = 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 = 81$
- $10^3 = 10 \cdot 10 \cdot 10 = 1000$

---

## Special Cases

### Zero Exponent
$$\boxed{a^0 = 1 \quad \text{for any } a \neq 0}$$

**Examples:**
- $5^0 = 1$
- $(xyz)^0 = 1$
- $(-42)^0 = 1$

### First Power
$$\boxed{a^1 = a}$$

**Example:** $7^1 = 7$

### Negative Exponents
$$\boxed{a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n} \quad \text{for } a \neq 0}$$

**Examples:**
- $2^{-3} = \frac{1}{2^3} = \frac{1}{8}$
- $10^{-2} = \frac{1}{100} = 0.01$
- $x^{-1} = \frac{1}{x}$

### Fractional Exponents
$$\boxed{a^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{a^m} = (\sqrt[n]{a})^m}$$

**Examples:**
- $8^{\frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{8} = 2$
- $16^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{16} = 4$
- $27^{\frac{2}{3}} = (\sqrt[3]{27})^2 = 3^2 = 9$
- $x^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{x}$

---

## Laws of Exponents

### 1. Product Rule
When multiplying with the same base, **add** the exponents:
$$\boxed{a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}}$$

**Examples:**
- $2^3 \cdot 2^4 = 2^{3+4} = 2^7 = 128$
- $x^5 \cdot x^3 = x^8$
- $10^2 \cdot 10^{-3} = 10^{-1} = 0.1$

---

### 2. Quotient Rule
When dividing with the same base, **subtract** the exponents:
$$\boxed{\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n} \quad \text{for } a \neq 0}$$

**Examples:**
- $\frac{5^7}{5^3} = 5^{7-3} = 5^4 = 625$
- $\frac{x^8}{x^2} = x^6$
- $\frac{2^5}{2^7} = 2^{-2} = \frac{1}{4}$

---

### 3. Power Rule
When raising a power to another power, **multiply** the exponents:
$$\boxed{(a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}}$$

**Examples:**
- $(2^3)^4 = 2^{12} = 4096$
- $(x^2)^5 = x^{10}$
- $(10^{-2})^3 = 10^{-6}$

---

### 4. Power of a Product
$$\boxed{(ab)^n = a^n \cdot b^n}$$

**Examples:**
- $(2 \cdot 3)^4 = 2^4 \cdot 3^4 = 16 \cdot 81 = 1296$
- $(xy)^5 = x^5y^5$
- $(2x^2)^3 = 2^3 \cdot (x^2)^3 = 8x^6$

---

### 5. Power of a Quotient
$$\boxed{\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^n = \frac{a^n}{b^n} \quad \text{for } b \neq 0}$$

**Examples:**
- $\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 = \frac{2^3}{3^3} = \frac{8}{27}$
- $\left(\frac{x}{y}\right)^4 = \frac{x^4}{y^4}$
- $\left(\frac{5}{2}\right)^{-2} = \frac{5^{-2}}{2^{-2}} = \frac{2^2}{5^2} = \frac{4}{25}$

---

### 6. Negative Exponent of Quotient
$$\boxed{\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^{-n} = \left(\frac{b}{a}\right)^n}$$

**Example:**
- $\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{-2} = \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{9}{4}$

## Scientific Notation

Scientific notation uses powers of 10 to express very large or small numbers:
$$\boxed{a \times 10^n}$$
where $1 \leq |a| < 10$ and $n$ is an integer.

**Examples:**
- $3,000,000 = 3 \times 10^6$
- $0.00045 = 4.5 \times 10^{-4}$
- Speed of light: $c \approx 3 \times 10^8$ m/s
- Planck's constant: $h \approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s

## Common Patterns

### Powers of 2
- $2^1 = 2$
- $2^2 = 4$
- $2^3 = 8$
- $2^4 = 16$
- $2^5 = 32$
- $2^6 = 64$
- $2^7 = 128$
- $2^8 = 256$
- $2^{10} = 1024$

### Powers of 10
- $10^{-3} = 0.001$ (milli-)
- $10^{-2} = 0.01$ (centi-)
- $10^{-1} = 0.1$ (deci-)
- $10^0 = 1$
- $10^1 = 10$ (deca-)
- $10^2 = 100$ (hecto-)
- $10^3 = 1000$ (kilo-)
- $10^6 = 1,000,000$ (mega-)

## Complex Examples

### Example 1: Simplify
$$\frac{(2x^3y^{-2})^4 \cdot x^{-5}}{(xy)^3}$$

**Solution:**
$$
\begin{align*}
&= \frac{2^4 \cdot x^{12} \cdot y^{-8} \cdot x^{-5}}{x^3 \cdot y^3} \\
&= \frac{16 \cdot x^{7} \cdot y^{-8}}{x^3 \cdot y^3} \\
&= 16 \cdot x^{4} \cdot y^{-11} \\
&= \frac{16x^4}{y^{11}}
\end{align*}
$$

### Example 2: Solve for x
$$2^{x+1} = 32$$

**Solution:**
$$
\begin{align*}
2^{x+1} &= 2^5 \\
x + 1 &= 5 \\
x &= 4
\end{align*}
$$

### Example 3: Evaluate
$$\left(\frac{27}{8}\right)^{-\frac{2}{3}}$$

**Solution:**
$$
\begin{align*}
\left(\frac{27}{8}\right)^{-\frac{2}{3}} &= \left(\frac{8}{27}\right)^{\frac{2}{3}} \\
&= \left(\sqrt[3]{\frac{8}{27}}\right)^2 \\
&= \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^2 \\
&= \frac{4}{9}
\end{align*}
$$

## Applications

### 1. Compound Interest
$$A = P\left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}$$

### 2. Population Growth
$$P(t) = P_0 \cdot e^{rt}$$

### 3. Radioactive Decay
$$N(t) = N_0 \cdot e^{-\lambda t}$$

### 4. Computer Science
- Binary: Powers of 2
- Storage: $2^{10} = 1024$ bytes = 1 KB

## Common Mistakes

1. **$a^m \cdot b^m \neq (ab)^{2m}$** ✗
   - Correct: $a^m \cdot b^m = (ab)^m$ ✓

2. **$(a + b)^2 \neq a^2 + b^2$** ✗
   - Correct: $(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$ ✓

3. **$a^m + a^n \neq a^{m+n}$** ✗
   - Cannot simplify addition of powers ✓

4. **$\frac{1}{a^{-n}} \neq a^{-n}$** ✗
   - Correct: $\frac{1}{a^{-n}} = a^n$ ✓
